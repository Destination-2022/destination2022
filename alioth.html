Destination 2022

Alioth

Planet type: Contains large patches of crater-less terrain, extreme temperatures Alioth is an inhabited rocky plant. Its atmosphere is extremely thin. Atmospheric composition is given as follows
• Oxygen 60% 
• Carbon 20% 
• Hydrogen 6% 
• Helium 9% 
• Methane 3.5% 
• Sodium – 1.3%      
• Other - 0.2%

Alioth’s surface temperatures are both extremely hot and cold. Alioth is the fastest planet, zipping around the Sun every 88 Earth days. Alioth's environment is not conducive to life as we know it. The temperatures and solar radiation that characterise this planet are most likely too extreme for organisms to adapt to. Instead of an atmosphere, Alioth possesses a thin exosphere made up of atoms blasted off the surface by the solar wind and striking meteoroids. Alioth's exosphere is composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium.Alioth's magnetic field is offset relative to the planet's equator. Though Alioth's magnetic field at the surface has just 1% the strength of Earth's, it interacts with the magnetic field of the solar wind to sometimes create intense magnetic tornadoes that funnel the fast, hot solar wind plasma down to the surface of the planet. When the ions strike the surface, they knock off neutrally charged atoms and send them on a loop high into the sky.       

Questions: Choose any two of three


1. Alioth has abundant helium. In a hypothetical situation if earth runs out of helium how would you get helium from Alioth keeping in mind that it's closest to earth. (Helium is used in making fibre optics, semiconductors, arc welding etc)

2. What would happen if you nuke Alioth? Would nuking it make any difference to the solar system?

3. It is found that it rains Sulphuric acid in Alioth but scientists have found that carbon exists in its  crystalline form. When the thick methane gas in the atmosphere goes through a lightning storm, the carbon gets converted into soot. As this soot falls down, due to the intense pressure of the exosphere, it gets converted into graphite and then diamond.  
As scientists of a space research organisation you are asked to find ways to get the diamonds in Alioth to earth. What would you do? (Keeping in mind Alioth has two permanent polar ice caps)
