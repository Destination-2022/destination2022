Destination 2022

Coeus


Type: Dry, Desert type planet
(pronounced “kee-yus”)

This is an Extraterrestrial Star-Planet System
 with a dimly orange glowing exo-planet to it, 
having a surface temperature between 80-90 
Celcius due to its close distance to its
 corresponding parent star (107.6 x 10^6 km)
 and Greenhouse trapping effect.The star is 
a white dwarf, class G star with mass of 
1.989 × 10^30 kgThe surface is desolate and
 continent-like features exist complete with
 canyons, mountains, plains, waterless
 oceans and a few relatively small craters.

It has a thick atmosphere made up almost entirely of carbon dioxide (Carbon Dioxide 56.3%, Oxygen-15.2%, Nitrogen 28.5%) . It also exerts a pressure 9 times higher than the earth's leading to an almost ‘pressure cooker’ environment.The planet is relatively similar to earth, size-wise, with a diameter at equator of 12,000km (approx) and Mass of 4.867 × 10^24 kg.Acceleration due to gravity - 817cm/sq.secThough the planet shares a lot of physical similarity with earth, its atmosphere’s bulk consists of clouds  H2S(g) which makes it toxic for human habitability. The clouds are so thick making it impossible to see its surface without using sophisticated radar systems.

However, Despite the corrosive atmosphere, after multiple missions of probes and rovers (beyond the technological advancement of Perseverance) having been sent to the planet, a revolutionary discovery was made.Traces of microbial life have been found within the subsurface and deep subsurface of the poles of the planet as the first ever form of life outside Earth!Along with which, micro quantities of water were present within the same crust.

Questions:

1. Why this Star-Planet system over others?

2. Do you, as scientists of a space research organisation, think that further manned missions (with advance sterilizable robotic instruments capable of taking microbial samples without risking the exo-species) should be sent to the planet in order to examine this discovery of extraterrestrial life or, to quote Carl Sagan, the planet is be left alone? Consider the ethical aspects of this question. 
If yes, How would you proceed examining this discovery of extraterrestrial life?
